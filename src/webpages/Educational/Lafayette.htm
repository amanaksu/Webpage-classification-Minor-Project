Treasuring

Learning

Ragin'

Cypress Lake is a treasured campus landmark. It is a carefully controlled and delicate wetland teeming with plants and wildlife such as bass, catfish, red-ear turtles, blue herons, and of course, alligators.

UL Lafayette was the first university in the country with such a wetland on campus.

Treasuring

Learning

Ragin'

Students at UL Lafayette have the greatest classroom in the world for learning about wetland ecosystems and management—Cypress Lake.

By observing, examining, and engaging with this environment, students understand the balance of nature and what humans can do to protect it—and help it thrive.

Learning

Ragin'

Every year students, faculty, and staff gather for canoe races in Cypress Lake during Lagniappe Week. Two per canoe, they paddle as quickly as they can through the water, around the cypress trees, and back to shore.

We are Ragin' Cajuns

Our students come from across the state and nation — and from more than 80 foreign countries. But here at UL Lafayette, we're all Ragin' Cajuns.

Community Spirit

As members of the campus community, we share great food and music and fresh ideas, too. Students are engaged in fun activities and interdisciplinary studies.

2,600 of us—including all freshmen—live on campus

Our campus is a neighborhood with riveting classes, energizing arts, ragin’ sports, and 185 student organizations.  

Join, serve, play

From Americorps to the Ragin’ Jazz dance team, 21 Greek organizations to the Nepal Students’ Association, we have an organization for you to make a difference.

Enjoy Our Neighborhood

Before, after, and between classes, stroll beneath 100-year-old oaks or visit Cypress Lake, a managed wetland on campus. Just don't feed the alligators!

Pass a Good Time

Our Cajun culture embraces hard work and dedication, but we also like to play and enjoy time together. Dancing, music, and good food are always in abundance here. So come by and, as we like to say, pass a good time.

Something Extra

On Lagniappe Day, take a break from studying. Have some well-deserved run and race across the lake. FYI: lagniappe means "an extra or unexpected gift."

Peel, eat, love

Lagniappe Day also means a serving of boiled crawfish. Every year, we prepare more than 15,000 pounds of Louisiana's tasty crustacean.

Red rules

We know the rules! Show your Ragin' Cajun spirit and wear red every Friday. Students, faculty and staff — just about everyone in town — are proud to wear red.

Hot Property

A major campus thoroughfare is known as Wear Red Avenue—just one of the hot spots on campus.

Everyday is a red-letter day

That’s because our student-athletes are impressive competitors on the field and in the classroom. More than half have a 3.0 GPA or higher.

See Our Colleges

Our schools and colleges emphasize interdisciplinary learning and offer a wide range of degrees, including highly regarded programs such as nursing, industrial design and engineering, and one-of-a-kind offerings such as traditional music. We also have opportunities to learn online.

Find Your Major

Our academic programs are continually evaluated and updated to ensure graduates a strong academic foundation and the best opportunities for jobs. Find the major that's right for you.

Graduate School

Ready for another degree? Our graduate school offers master's and doctoral programs, as well as graduate certificates to help you aim even higher.

ULearn Online

With our online programs, you'll experience the same level of attention from our exceptional faculty. Our robust online courses are designed to prepare you for the next step in your career—on your schedule.

Learn About Cost

We provide a high-quality education at a reasonable cost. UL Lafayette is the most affordable university in the state and among the top 10 percent of most affordable four-year public universities nationwide, according to information released in 2013 by the U.S. Department of Education.
