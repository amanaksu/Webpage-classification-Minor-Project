  • Cleary University set to celebrate 130th anniversary with Founder’s Week events

    HOWELL – Cleary University is celebrating its 130th anniversary Oct. 10-13 and the party includes something for just about everyone.

    Events include: an Entrepreneur’s Forum featuring local business leaders, the Livingston Economic Club Luncheon, a community-wide picnic, intercollegiate athletics, a disc golf tournament and the dedication of a new mural by a world-renown Michigan artist.

  • WW II author brings personal stories to October LEC

    HOWELL – World War II author and executive director of the George S. Patton Foundation, Robert H. Miller, will be the keynote speaker at the final Livingston Economic Club Speaker Series event Oct. 10.

    The event is Thursday, Oct. 10, 11:30 a.m.-2 p.m. at the Johnson Center on the campus of Cleary University. Tickets are $45 or $320 for a table of eight. Tickets are available at www.cleary.edu/lec/tickets.

    “Our final speaker is just as powerful as our first two,” said Grace Farley, coordinator of the event. “Many of us have veterans in our families and we don’t know much about what they went through or go through during their time in military service.”

  • Cleary names Kling head coach of new baseball program

    HOWELL – Howell resident Karl Kling has been named the head coach of Cleary University’s new baseball program.

    “To be a part of the hometown university is thrilling,” said Kling. “We can create our own history. You don’t get to do that that often.”

    “Karl Kling brings to a wealth of baseball knowledge and coaching experience to Cleary,” said Dan Riggs, director of athletics at Cleary University. “We feel that he will establish a quality baseball program.”

    Cleary University recently added baseball, softball and lacrosse to its athletic program. Cleary currently has men’s and women’s golf, soccer and cross country. The three new sports will begin play in 2015. MORE

  • Norris, Jennings receive All-American golf honors

    STATE COLLEGE, Pa. – The Cleary University men’s golf team finished fifth overall at the United States Collegiate Athletic Association (USCAA) Golf Championships at Penn State Tuesday.

    Randy Norris shot two-round 152 to finished in ninth place individually. The finish netted Norris 2013 USCAA Golf Second Team All-American honors.

    Gary Jennings was named to the 2013 USCAA All-Academic Team.

  • Norris helps keep Cleary University in hunt at USCAA Golf Championship

    STATE COLLEGE, Pa. – Cleary University’s Randy Norris, ranked 10th in the nation, was one of only four golfers to shoot par on any nine holes in the first round of the 2013 USCAA Golf National Championships.

    Norris shot a 6-over 78 on the par 72 blue course at Penn State to help Cleary to seventh place with one more round to play. The Cougars combined for a first-round 333 (+45). Daemen College leads the event with a team score of 297 (+9). Rochester College is currently third with a team score of 310 (+22).
