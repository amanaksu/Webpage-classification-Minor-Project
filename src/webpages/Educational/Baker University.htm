Baker alumnus transitions to director of facilities at Kauffman Center

Bill Miller, '90, has transferred the skills gained during 20 years in education to operating the
285,000-square-foot facility that hosts several hundred events a year. Read More

The new dining hall, phase one of the Student Union renovation, features stations similar to the food court at a shopping mall. Students, faculty and staff have several daily options, including pizza and pasta, classic entrees, vegetarian, grilled food, international, dessert and beverages. A $1.1 million lead gift from donor Susanne Richardson Teel spearheaded the project.

"I feel the environment is more in sync with college life," said Vincent Tadokoro, a junior from Lee's Summit, Mo. "The atmosphere is more cafe-ish. I love the layout because it is more open. You can picture yourself spending a lot of time here."

Megan Rosa, a senior from Olathe, Kan., is thrilled to enjoy the dining hall during her final year at Baker. She especially liked the larger coffee mug to start her day.

"It is awe-inspiring and so different from my first three years here," Rosa said. "I love the new furniture and new layout. It seems more like Baker with the orange tile and furniture. I am impressed the project was completed in a short amount of time. Since I was here before the renovation, I will appreciate this area so much more. This will be a great recruiting tool."

"This is an amazing facility and will change Baker forever," Baker President Pat Long said. "I am so grateful for all of our donors. We did it right. Walking in and seeing the students' reaction make it all worth it. We needed a place for our students to hang out and gather. We feel this is the place."
