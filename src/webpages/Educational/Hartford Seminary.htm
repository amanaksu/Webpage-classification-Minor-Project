Faculty In The News

Prof. Shanell Smith will preach on "The Post-Harvest Syndrome: What Do We Do with God's Blessings Once the Hype Is Over?" at St. Stephen’s Episcopal Church in Bloomfield on Sunday, Oct. 13, at 4 p.m. as part of the church's Annual Harvest Festival. 

At the invitation of Şirnak University in Turkey, Prof. Yahya Michot participated in the International Symposium on Noah and Judi Mountain last month. Prof. Michot delivered a paper titled "Did God Cry after the Flood? Classical Islamic Theology Debates about Noah, his Ark and his Family." He also chaired one of the sessions and spoke in the final panel. The event was covered by several news organizations, including the Star Gazette, Haberturk and Haberler.

Prof. Steve Blackburn has been elected to the executive board of the American Association of Teachers of Arabic. Prof. Blackburn, who studied in Tunisia and Cairo as an undergraduate at Georgetown University, said he can trace his interest in Arabic to the stories his grandparents would tell about living in North Africa. Prof. Blackburn will travel to New Orleans in October to attend a meeting of the organization. "It's an honor," he said of his election. "The names of the past presidents are legendary in the field, and here I am on this board. It's indescribable."   

Prof. Timur Yuskaev, co-director of the Islamic Chaplaincy program, was quoted in an Associated Press article called "Demand for U.S.-Born Imams Up in American Mosques." The article was picked up by more than a dozen news outlets, including NBC News.

Prof. Miriam Therese Winter has a number of appearances planned for the fall, including a program on "Women in the Early Church" at 7 p.m., Oct. 3, at St. Thomas the Apostle Church in West Hartford, a talk on "Unleashing the Spirit. The Inside Story," at the National Call to Action Conference in Milwaukee on Nov. 3 at 9:45 a.m., and program on "Quantum Spirit, Inclusive Spirituality" at Holy Names University in Oakland, Calif., on Dec. 6 and 7.

Faculty Publication

Prof. Lucinda Mosher is the guest editor of the latest issue of the journal Teaching Theology & Religion, a special issue on Multifaith Theological Education. Both Prof. Mosher and Prof. Timur Yuskaev have articles published in the journal. Prof. Yuskaev's article is called "Staying Put: Local Context as Classroom for Multifaith Education," and Prof. Mosher's article is "Meeting the Familiar Yet Strange: Strategies for Introducing American Christians to Jesus and Mary and Muslims Know Them." 

Prof. Najib George Awad's article, “When Identity is Constructed on a Procrustean Bed ‘Arab Christianity’ and the Missionary-Planted Churches in the Eyes of Muslim Authors,” has been published in Zur Situation der Christen in der Tüekei und in Syrien: Exemplarische Einsichten, Martin Tamcke (ed.), Wiesbaden: Harrassowitz Verlag, 2013.

Prof. Timur Yuskaev has an article included in The Cambridge Companion to American Islam (Cambridge University Press, 2013). His article is "Muslim Public Intellectuals and Global Muslim Thought."

Prof. Lucinda Mosher contributed a chapter to Christian-Muslim Relations in the Anglican and Lutheran Communions. Her chapter is titled "Renewing Our Pledge: The Episcopal Church's Engagement with Islam and Muslims."

Co-Directors Named for Islamic Chaplaincy Program

Prof. Feryal Salem will join Prof. Timur Yuskaev as the co-director of the Islamic Chaplaincy Program, Hartford Seminary President Heidi Hadsell has announced. 

The Seminary's Islamic Chaplaincy Program trains Muslim chaplains for work in institutions such as colleges and universities, hospitals, prisons, and the military. The impetus for the program came in the 1990s after the U.S. government approached the Seminary asking for such a program to address the need for Muslim chaplains in the military. Hartford Seminary's Islamic Chaplaincy Program, officially launched in 2003, remains the only accredited such program in the United States,

C.L. Seow Lecture Draws Standing-Room Only Crowd

A lecture by acclaimed scholar C.L. Seow drew a large crowd to Hartford Seminary on Sept. 30 to hear about "Job's Wife in Jewish, Muslim and Christian Interpretations."

C.L. Seow is Henry Snyder Gehman Professor of Old Testament Language and Literature at Princeton Theological Seminary. A recognized expert in Old Testament studies and the general editor of the Illuminations series, he is also the author of the Anchor Bible commentary on Ecclesiastes.

Through a training partnership with the National Disaster Interfaiths Network (NDIN), Hartford Seminary will host a Disaster Chaplain training course on the Hartford Seminary campus on Tuesday and Wednesday, Nov. 5 and 6, from 8:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. Congregation leaders, chaplains, spiritual care providers, mental health professionals, and emergency managers are urged to enroll.

Lecture Series to Explore East Windsor Hill Theological Seminary

East Windsor Hill Theological Seminary, the founding institution that is now Hartford Seminary, was located in South Windsor from 1834 to 1865. The Wood Memorial Library in South Windsor is conducting a series of three lectures, a walking tour and a gallery exhibit to explore the insitution's controversial beginnings and its influence on the town. Three Hartford seminary professors will participate in the series.

Seminary Makes Impressive Showing at ISNA

Hartford Seminary was well represented at the 50th annual convention of the Islamic Society of North America in Washington, DC, over Labor Day weekend.  Sohaib Sultan, ’10, now Muslim Chaplain at Princeton University, was Program Committee co-chair for the convention, which drew thousands to the annual offering of lectures on religious, social and spiritual topics, opportunities for networking, and the extensive bazaar of booths populated by educational institutions, social organizations, and merchants.  Many visited the Seminary’s booth, including prospective students.
