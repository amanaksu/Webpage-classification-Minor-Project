News

Supporting student innovation for positive change UAB has joined the Clinton Global Initiative University Network, a national consortium of institutions that support, mentor and provide seed funding to student innovators and entrepreneurs. President Bill Clinton launched the CGI U Network in 2007 to engage the next generation of college leaders worldwide in developing plans and taking action to create positive change.

“CGI U is a great platform for our UAB student innovators, many of whom are already collaborating with local and global partners to solve problems,” says Libba Vaughan, director of UAB’s Office of Service Learning. “CGI U inspires students to take action and share their positive impact in the world, even before they graduate.”

What do you want to be? Do you want to change the world? In a new ad now running on TV screens across the state, UAB undergraduates encourage prospective students to turn their passion into action. The 30-second commercial, titled "Where knowledge begins," launches the second phase in UAB's branding campaign, "Knowledge that will change your world."

Asia Sullivan, a sophomore honors student, delivers the video's opening lines at the Alys Stephens Center. Her fellow students are then shown at locations across campus, from Heritage Hall and the Campus Green to UAB Hospital and Sterne Library. Sullivan is excited to be a face of the new campaign. “UAB has not only provided me with a stellar education thus far, but has given me a family,” she says.

Learn more about the student experience at uab.edu/students.
