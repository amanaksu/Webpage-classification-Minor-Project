Disciples of the Lord: Missionary Priests for the New Evangelization

  • A Message From the Rector

    • August 22, 2013
      Queenship of Mary

      Dear Friends in Christ:

      Notre Dame Seminary began its 90th anniversary welcoming 26 new seminarians to our community and welcoming back our returning seminarians bringing our enrollment to 96 this year, up 10% from last year. Certainly it is not about numbers rather it is about the quality of the candidates and the formation program. This year NDS once again offers our seminarians a fine and competent faculty to serve their needs with the appointment of a new vice rector, academic dean, director of pre-theology, director of sacred liturgy, and director of homiletics. These appointments as well as the work of our current faculty positions NDS to be the finest seminary in the United States.

      As we celebrate our 90th anniversary this year, the formation program continues to bring before the seminarians an emphasis on the “missionary priest.” Considering the recent Synod of Bishops on the new evangelization and Pope Francis’ first encyclical on Faith, the priest of today’s world needs to be prepared to meet every human being wherever that person finds themselves preaching and living the Gospel in a credible and effective way. This is what NDS is all about – preparing seminarians for the new evangelization, to be missionary priests in their own dioceses and religious congregations.

      We ask for your prayers and support this year. We ask that Our Lady, the Queen of Heaven and Earth, intercede for us and help us all to be faithful in our ministry and vocation. As the Church celebrates this Year of Faith, may our seminarians embrace the call of the Lord as they prepare to serve the People of God.

      Sincerely yours in the Lord,

      Very Reverend James A. Wehner
      Rector/President
