The Washington Theological Union is a Roman Catholic graduate school of theology and ministry. The Union participates in the mission of the Church in its pursuit of truth and integration of knowledge for the common good. Sponsored by the religious communities and informed by their charisms, the Union educates for ordination, lay ministry and leadership in the Church and world. The school will be concluding its 45 years of service in 2013.

Union Alumna Receives John Hines Preaching Award

  By Marc DelMonico | July 22, 2013 - 11:49 am | Front Page, News-Events
Comments Off

The Rev. Catherine D. Hicks, the Rector at St. Peter’s Episcopal Church in Port Royal, Virginia, has been named recipient of Virginia Theological Seminary’s 2013 John Hines Preaching Award. The award is given annually to the outstanding preaching entry “where prophetic voice is central within the sermon.”

Rev. Hicks received her Spiritual Direction Studies certificate from the Washington Theological Union.

Recent Union D.Min. Graduate Authors Book on Ecumenical Spirituality

  By Marc DelMonico | July 11, 2013 - 1:55 pm | Front Page, News-Events
Comments Off

Otis Edgar Hamm, Jr., who received his Doctor of Ministry Degree from Washington Theological Union, has recently published a book that has been credited as a “valuable model for teaching ecumenical spirituality.” With a forward to the book provided by Union Scripture professor Rev. Joseph Wimmer, O.S.A., Hamm’s book, Gates Unlatched: An Ecumenical Spirituality Within the Academy focuses on efforts in Protestant theological education to attend to the method with which to effectively teach courses related to spiritual formation.

Hamm is presently an Adjunct Professor of Religion at Mount Olive College in Mount Olive, North Carolina and specializes in Christian spirituality. Gates Unlatched is published by WIPF & Stock (Eugene, Oregon).

Union Celebrates Final Graduation Ceremony

  By Marc DelMonico | May 18, 2013 - 10:47 am | Front Page, News-Events
Comments Off

May 19, 2013

Dear Students, Colleagues and Friends of the Washington Theological Union,

A clear azure blue sky, soft breeze and the sun’s warmth and energy provided a memorable setting for the Forty Fourth and final graduation ceremony of our beloved Washington Theological Union on Friday, May 3, 2013 at St. Camillus Church, Silver Spring, MD. Fifty three students from the class of 2013 received degrees or certificates unique to their respective disciplines of study. The music provided by the St, Camillus Multicultural Choir under the direction of Ms. Tracy McDonnell, carried the theme of Wisdom for the graduation. The Rev. Andrew D. Ciferni, O.Praem., Ph.D. delivered a moving address to the graduates pointing to the beauty of a starlit Vatican II Council noting its documents and their prophetic and timeless brilliance. It was particularly meaningful for this graduation because it was a reminder of the impetus behind the very opening of the Union in 1968 in which the architectural design of the curriculum under the leadership of the first academic dean, Rev. Michael Scanlon, O.S.A., was guided by the great inspiration of the Second Vatican Council. Stephen R. Sohe, MA, MDIV, offered a student reflection on the experience of studying at the Union and how the sense of community drew him to a greater appreciation of Wisdom through the faculty, his colleagues and through the gift of his Nigerian ancestry. The celebration continued with a magnificent reception in the Camilla Hall of St. Camillus. The St Camillus Community was most hospitable in hosting the event. For all of these good things, we give thanks!

A gallery of images from the graduation is posted below.

As we celebrate the Feast of Pentecost, let us pray, “In the quick rushing wind of the Spirit, may justice prevail. In the cool gentle breeze of the Sprit, may the Mercy of God enfold us, and in the enduring fire of the Spirit’s love for each of us, ever hasten peace in our world. Amen.”

With warm regards,

Anne E. McLaughlin, R.S.M., D.Min.
Chief Academic Officer

  • Recent Projects

    The Washingon Theological Union, with the support of a grant from the John Templeton Foundation, initiated a program to encourage a lively conversation between scientists and theologians on big issues of common interest such as creation, evolution, and spirituality. Under the leadership of Rev. Joseph F. Wimmer, S.T.D. and Daryl P. Domning, Ph.D., four public conferences featuring scientists and theologians were held between fall 2011 and spring 2013 on various issues of the interaction between science and religion. Entitled the atom + Eve Project, the results are available to all on their website.
