Biggest Snowball Honor Returns to Michigan Tech

Michigan Tech has made the Guinness Book of World Records again. Last winter, a team of mechanical engineering students rolled a snowball almost 33 feet in circumference—the biggest snowball in the world—taking back a world record. Their accomplishment was confirmed by Guinness this month.

To the Moon

For the last several years, PhD graduate Brandon Dilworth has poured all his professional skill and passion into a game-changing scientific project that is now hitching a ride on the Lunar Atmosphere and Dust Environment Explorer spacecraft. LADEE (dubbed "laddie") entered lunar orbit Oct. 6, and if all goes well, Dilworth's project could help revolutionize space communication.

Electrical Engineer Turns Particle Physics into Art

A Michigan Tech alumnus is fusing science and creativity, using a high-energy particle accelerator to craft pieces of art. Todd Johnson, a special projects specialist at Fermilab—a national laboratory in Illinois—says he's fascinated by the relationship of art to science.

Smile!

More than dentures or bridges, implants mimic the look and feel of natural teeth. Still, they are costly, and a small percentage either fall out or must be removed. Michigan Tech scientist Tolou Shokuhfar wants to lower that failure rate to zero by using nanotechnology.

Generations of Discovery Campaign Meets and Beats Its Goal

The goal was $200 million, but MIchigan Tech didn't raise $200 million during its Generation of Discoveries capital campaign. The University raised more than that—a total of $215,366,353.
