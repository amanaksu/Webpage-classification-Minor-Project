Fr. Cessario to Lecture on Catholicism in the 21st Century

The public is invited to hear Rev. Romanus Cessario, O.P., (STB ‘70, STL ‘72) give a lecture entitled Culture and Contemplation: Catholicism in the 21st Century at the PFIC on November 11, 2013. The lecture will be delivered on the occasion of his receiving the degree and title Master of Sacred Theology (S.T.M.) and in observance of the feast of St. Albert the Great. The S.T.M. is the highest honor given by the Dominican Order, and is bestowed upon a friar for his extraordinary theological contributions.

Fr. Cessario is a Professor of Systematic Theology at St. John’s Seminary School of Theology in Brighton, Massachusetts. He is the author of numerous books, Senior Editor of Magnificat, and Associate Editor of The Thomist. His extensive experience includes fifteen years teaching Systematic Theology at the PFIC.

The lecture will be given in the Academic Center’s Aquin Hall at 7:30 p.m. on Monday, November 11, and will be followed by a reception. No RSVP is necessary.
