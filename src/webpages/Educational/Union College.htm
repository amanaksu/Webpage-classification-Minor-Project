Union College

Union College is an accredited, Seventh-day Adventist institution of higher learning in Lincoln, Nebraska. Founded in 1891, Union offers university-level degrees with more than 50 majors, minors and emphases in 27 fields of study.

Alumnus credits IRR degree for a career of service and adventure

A 2009 international rescue and relief graduate, Jonathan Hoewing found the degree and experiences he gained at Union College open doors around the world as he's worked in Haiti, Saudi Arabia and now Papua New Guinea.

Find out more.

 

Our Promising Future

Five years ago, the $14.5 million to build Union's new math and science center was a daunting goal. Thanks to a $500,000 challenge by alumnus Steve Gifford in honor of his wife, Beverly, that goal may be met by the end of December.

Find out how you can help.

 

Project Impact 2013

Each year, Union College students and employees take a day off of classes to give back to Lincoln, assisting non-profits who help others all year. It's more than just one day, it's a way for students to find long-term volunteer projects and continue Union's culture of service.

Read more.

 

Newest programs, new facility

The southern wing of the Don Love Building has a new look and a new function. The renovated space is now the home of the physician assistant studies and international rescue and relief programs, giving a new home to two of Union's newest programs.

Read more.
