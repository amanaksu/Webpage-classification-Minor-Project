  • Value

    Lyon College provides each student with an affordable, enriching education.  Nearly all incoming students receive scholarships and other types of financial aid to help fund their education.  Graduates complete their degree with the lowest debt per student in Arkansas.

  • Tradition

    Founded in 1872, Lyon College is the oldest independent college in Arkansas in the second oldest city in the state. From the Greek system to athletics and the Scottish heritage program, Lyon aims to instill a sense of history and tradition in every facet of campus life.

  • Opportunity

    Students have countless opportunities, even after they graduate. Students can travel the world with the Nichols International Studies Program and strengthen their minds with rigorous classes. A Lyon education opens the door to many different career paths, research positions, and post-graduate programs.

  • Quality

    We believe education is more than a degree. With a student-faculty ratio of 15:1, Lyon College provides a personalized education unlike any other. Students are taught by leaders in their respective field, not graduate assistants, and students can participate in collaborative research.

  • Community

    Lyon is a community, distinguished by its academic curriculum, unique Honor and Social Systems and award-winning professors. As one of the premier liberal arts colleges in the South, Lyon strives to create a learning environment that is academically challenging and indicative of building character and integrity.

  • Adventure

    Lyon College’s unique outdoor program offers students the chance to explore the environment around them and go beyond their comfort zones. Lyon’s beautiful 136-acre campus includes a bouldering wall, disc golf course, hiking and biking trail and ropes course.

Shaw shares passion for marine biology

Posted on Monday, October 7

Inspired by one of her favorite movies, Lyon College senior biology major Stephanie Shaw has had a passion for marine mammals for most of her life. “When I was two years old, I watched “Free Willy” for the first time,” Shaw said. “That’s when I fell in love with orcas. From there, my love for ocean life continued to grow.”  The story about Keiko, the killer whale from Free Willy, and his release into the wild, inspired Shaw to pursue a career in marine biology.

 

Vestal selected for Leadership Arkansas

Posted on Monday, October 7

Lyon College Vice President for Institutional Advancement Jon Vestal has been selected to participate in Leadership Arkansas, a program created by the Arkansas State Chamber of Commerce and Associated Industries of Arkansas and designed to show the economic and political challenges that face Arkansas.

Leadership Arkansas sessions include a nine-month class, sessions held in communities throughout the state, exposure to social and environmental complexities of the state, on-site demonstrations and tour and insight into influences on public policies.
