Emily Burdeshaw '13

Life-Long Friendships. Career Preparation.




“I have complete confidence that the last four years have prepared me to succeed in my career and to constantly challenge myself to be better at what I do and who I am.”

View Profile >

Emily Burdeshaw '13

Hometown: Aberdeen, MD
High School: John Carroll (in Bel Air, MD)
Major: English
Minor: Studio Art

I love St. Mary’s because every fall and spring day is a barefoot day. Every sunny day is a Frisbee day. Every blustering winter’s day leads to a study date with hot tea and every Tuesday means the Farmer’s market provides ingredients for a meal that is fresh and local. I came to St. Mary’s because of a shoeless walk I took through campus on a sunny accepted students day. I stayed at St. Mary’s because I had the opportunity to learn with (and from) some of the most beautiful people I have ever met. Instances like a Point Lookout bike ride punctuated with a hearty philosophical debate, or a voluntary weekend art history field trip to DC when we met our professor for Chinese, convinced me that I would thrive at this small school on the river. Spirited conversations about Japanese culture and Thai yoga lifestyle with a CBS producer, and an internship that led to a job in Public Affairs convinced me I had chosen the best school to prepare me for the real world and to provide the best opportunity to succeed.

When I graduated, I thought I would feel apprehension about the future, particularly with regard to my friendships and career, but I didn’t. By May, I was ready to graduate, spread my wings. I had a job and the assurance that my mentors were an email away. My friends were in Baltimore or DC or a skype call away. I have complete confidence that the last four years have prepared me to succeed in my career and to constantly challenge myself to be better at what I do and who I am.

The DeSousa-Brent Scholars Program

The DeSousa-Brent Scholars Program cultivates the academic and leadership potential of talented students from groups traditionally underrepresented at St. Mary's. The select program prepares a community of scholars to guide the College's efforts to increase understanding of diversity and inclusion.
