Student wins prestigious fellowship, will travel to Saudi Arabia

Cody Knipfer, a junior at McDaniel College majoring in Political Science and International Studies, is one of 10 students from across the nation selected for the Saudi Arabia Exchange Fellowship. The Saudi Arabia Exchange Fellowship consists of an expense-paid study visit to Saudi Arabia in late December 2013 through early January 2014 for a cultural immersion experience.

The participants also engage in various outreach activities to share their experiences in the Saudi culture during the year-long fellowship from January through December 2014. Only 10 students from across the nation are selected for this program and are required to have previously participated in the National Council on U.S.-Arab Relations’ Model Arab League.

Fun with Code

Computer Science students are learning X-Code and Objective-C, the software tool and language of iOS applications, to collaborate on designing an assortment of games ranging from variations on Tetris to a Baseball-themed fantasy game. The finished products each feature hundreds of lines of code.

 

Their professor Sara More says she looks forward to exploring more features of mobile devices with students in the future, including location awareness capabilities and gesture options. The collaboration “mirrors real life because coding isn’t an isolated experience like it’s made out to be in the movies” she says. “Our students tend to work well together.”

Interns explore careers and enhance studies

During their summer internships, seniors Meghan Schatz and Jabraan Ismail sampled every aspect of video production, from operating cameras to lighting, audio and make-up to editing.

 

The result? Schatz’s experience at Maryland Public Television and Ismail’s work in Hollywood with McDaniel alumnus and Hallmark producer Jim Wilberger focused the Cinema majors’ career plans on becoming producers who do it all.

 

Almost half of McDaniel students complete at least one internship by the time they graduate. This test drive gives students the opportunity to explore careers and enhance classroom learning – and sometimes points their compass in a new direction.
